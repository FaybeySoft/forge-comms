# Proposal — evolve `forgebot`

**Filed by:** manager (Captain Holt)
**When:** 2026-05-12T21:57:07.864Z

## Rationale

palmares-barca shows multiple automatic escalations and triage issues (#21, #22, #24, #26). These escalations are creating follow-ups requiring human/legal review without a clear deliverable or PO confirmation, which increases noise and slows resolution.

## Proposed change

Replace escalation behavior with a two-step rule under "Escalations":

1) Require a concrete deliverable before escalation: do not open an issue tagged "urgent" or "legal" unless the bot attaches or links a concrete deliverable (one of: PR draft, reproducible test case, legal-risk brief with specific question). If none exists, open the issue as "needs-triage" and post a checklist outlining what is missing.

2) Require PO confirmation for legal escalations: when the bot determines an item may need legal review, it must: (a) add a short rationale (<= 3 sentences) and (b) ping the repo's Product Owner with a templated message asking for confirmation to escalate. Only after the PO responds "escalate" should the bot open a formal "URGENTE: Revisión legal" issue.

Prompt snippet to add:
"When preparing to open an escalation (labels: urgent, legal, compliance), check for a linked deliverable. If none exists, create an issue with label needs-triage and include this checklist: - Repro steps or PR draft attached? - Concrete ask defined? - Owner/PO identified? Then stop and notify the PO. Only open a labeled escalation after PO confirmation."

Evidence: palmares-barca issues #21, #22, #24, #26 and recent code-critic artifacts where bot-originated triage created noisy follow-ups.
